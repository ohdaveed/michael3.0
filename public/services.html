What You Need

Core Documents That Work Together

A solid plan layers a few key instruments so your care, money, and legacy stay aligned if something changes.

01 — Core Document

Living Trust

If your gross estate exceeds $208,850, or your primary residence exceeds $750,000, probate may apply—and statutory fees add up fast (often well above a trust package). A Living Trust lets successor trustees follow your instructions during incapacity and after death, usually without a public court file.

You will need to name two Successor Trustees.
02 — Included with Trust

Pour Over Will

Pairs with revocable trusts, names guardians for minors, and can sweep overlooked assets into your trust after death.

You will need to name Guardians for your children and two Executors.
03 — Included with Trust

General Durable Power of Attorney

Lets trusted agents pay bills and coordinate care using your written directions—without a conservatorship hearing.

You will need to name two alternate Agents.
04 — Included with Trust

Advance Health Care Directive

States who decides care if you cannot speak for yourself and records the treatments you want—or want to refuse.

You will need to name two alternate Agents for Health Care.

Beyond the Documents

A Few Details Worth Getting Right

Some assets need more than a checkbox. Here's what Michael looks at beyond the standard funding checklist.

Real Property

Real Estate & Deeds

Own property in California? Michael prepares and records the deed that moves it into your trust, and works through title, mortgage, and property-tax questions along the way.

One deed transfer is included with the Complete Living Trust Package; additional properties or complex title issues may require a separate fee.
Complex Assets

Business Interests & Complex Accounts

LLC interests, startup equity, and stock options each come with their own rules for how—or whether—they can be retitled into your trust, and Michael reviews yours for company restrictions before anything moves. Retirement accounts generally stay in your name instead, with the trust named as beneficiary.

Company documents or plan rules are checked before any business interest is transferred.
Married & Engaged Couples

Separate Property & Community Property

For married couples, Michael helps identify which assets are separate, community, or a mix of both—especially important for property owned before marriage, inheritances, or a business interest. Planning to marry? An estate plan can also help preserve a clear record of what's yours going in.

Especially relevant for assets acquired before marriage, inheritances, or business and startup equity.
