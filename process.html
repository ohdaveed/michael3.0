Our Process

What to Expect: Your Journey to Peace of Mind

Estate planning doesn't have to be complicated or stressful. Here's exactly how we'll work together to protect your legacy.

01

Free Initial Consultation

Duration: 45-60 minutes | Location: Our office, your home, or phone

We'll discuss your family situation, assets, goals, and concerns. You'll learn which documents you need, how the process works, and receive a clear, upfront price quote. No pressure, no obligation — just honest guidance.

Call (415) 596-6007 or Text Us →
02

Design Your Plan

Duration: 1-2 weeks | Format: Detailed questionnaire

After engagement, we'll send you a comprehensive questionnaire covering your assets, family situation, and wishes. This ensures we capture every important detail. We'll review your responses together and design a plan tailored precisely to your needs.

03

Draft & Review Documents

Duration: 2-3 weeks | Revisions: Unlimited

We draft your complete estate plan with meticulous attention to detail. You'll receive preliminary documents to review. We welcome questions and revisions — we'll refine everything until you're completely satisfied and understand every provision.

04

Signing Ceremony

Duration: 60-90 minutes | Location: Our office or your home

We'll walk through each document, explain key provisions, and answer final questions. You'll sign in the presence of witnesses and a notary. We can coordinate with spouses, trustees, or other signatories as needed.

05

Implementation & Asset Transfers

Duration: Ongoing support | Guidance: Step-by-step

For Living Trusts, we provide detailed instructions on transferring assets into the trust (real estate, bank accounts, investments). We can handle real estate transfers directly and guide you through other transfers. Your plan only works if properly funded.

06

Lifetime Support

Updates: As needed | Availability: Always here

Life changes — new children, marriages, divorces, asset changes, relocations. We're here to update your plan as needed. We'll also provide guidance to your trustees or executors when the time comes to administer your estate.
