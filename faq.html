California probate typically takes 9-18 months, sometimes longer if there are complications. The minimum statutory fees are based on estate value: $5,980 for a $184,500 estate, $13,000 for $500,000, and $23,000 for $1,000,000. These fees are paid to both the attorney and executor. A Living Trust avoids probate entirely, saving time and money.

A Will alone does not avoid probate. If your gross estate exceeds $184,500 (California's probate threshold), your heirs will face probate court regardless of having a Will. A Living Trust avoids probate entirely, saves substantial fees, maintains privacy, and provides better protection if you become incapacitated. For most homeowners in San Francisco, a Living Trust is essential.

A Revocable Living Trust can be amended or revoked at any time during your life — you maintain complete control. Most families use revocable trusts for probate avoidance and flexibility. An Irrevocable Trust cannot be changed once created, but offers additional asset protection and tax benefits. We'll help you determine which type fits your goals during your consultation.

Review your estate plan every 3-5 years or whenever major life changes occur: marriage, divorce, births, deaths, significant asset changes, relocations to another state, or changes in your wishes for trustees/beneficiaries. Without powers of attorney, your family must go to court to obtain a conservatorship — an expensive, public, and time-consuming process. The court appoints someone to manage your affairs, which may not be who you would have chosen. Powers of attorney allow your chosen agents to act immediately without court involvement, ensuring your wishes are followed.
